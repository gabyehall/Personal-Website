EnviroHacks

A Hackathon for high school students, by high school students

EnviroHacks was a project I developed over the course of my senior year. I developed this event as part of my internship with 01 after competing in the 2018 eMerge Americas hackathon. My team was made entirely of high school students, and we were the only team of our kind. While we did not win, we had a blast and we're proud to make it to the end of the competition, something that not all of the adult teams did. I was lucky enough to have a company backing my participation in a competition like this, and I wanted to give more students the same opportunity.

This event was a coding competition for high school students meant to encourage students to pursue computer science. For this event, I recruited sponsors, competitors, speakers, and did all of the organization. Ultimately, I had students representing schools all across Miami-Dade county compete. I raised over two thousand dollars of sponsorship money and provided the competitors with a venue, prizes, and food throughout the day. I also had laptops and other materials available.

The event was ultimately very successful. I was fortunate to receive recognition for my work. I was a Silver Knight honorable mention for the category of mathematics. I was recognized by the school board during my high school’s graduation ceremony. I was also recognized by the National Center for Women in Technology with their regional Aspirations in Computing Award and as an honorable mention for their national Aspirations in Computing Award.

Notorious S-H-E

Notorious S-H-E, a podcast by women in STEM for future women in STEM.

In this podcast, I interview women in STEM careers to expose listeners to different career options and resources. The goal of this podcast is to show young girls that anyone, regardless of their background, can be successful in their STEM careers. Each episode features a lightning lesson and an interview with a successful woman in STEM. These lightning lessons are meant to explain different STEM topics and highlight careers similar to that of the interviewee. I also built the podcast’s website (notoriousshe.com) which features episode-specific and general resources.

This podcast was developed in partnership with the Sanctuary for Independent Media’s Hudson Mowhawk radio station. The Hudson Mohawk Magazine is a locally produced news hour that provides sanctuary for the discussion of a wide range of ideas. The Hudson Mohawk Magazine advances the Sanctuary for Independent Media’s organizational mission to use art and participatory action to promote social and environmental justice and freedom of expression.

Cognitive Robotics Lab

As a project for my Minds and Machines class, I worked with a team in the cognitive robotics lab to develop a robot to complete the Wumpus challenge. For this project, I worked as a programmer for both the website and the actual robot.

The Wumpus World challenge is an academic endeavor that combines logic, engineering, and collaboration to solve a problem. Our team went about building and coding a robot to successfully navigate a grid full of potential hazards and achieve a set goal. In the Wumpus world challenge, there is a grid with a set amount of holes, 1 Wumpus monster, and 1 pile of gold. If our robot moved to an area containing a hole or the wumpus, we lost the challenge, but if we successfully navigated the grid and found the pile of gold, we won. We can not see where these things are, so we must rely on input from the environment. When a hole is near, a breeze is felt, when the Wumpus is near, there is a smell, and when the gold is near, we see the glitter. When we moved to a new square, we would be informed of any new input, which we would then send to the robot.
